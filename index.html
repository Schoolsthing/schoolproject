Overview & Food Insecurity

  • Mexico chosen for its food, warm climate, and vibrant culture
  • Top tourist spot: beaches, ruins, cuisine
  • Population ~130 million; capital is Mexico City
  • Spanish is spoken; uses Mexican peso
  • 30–60% experience food insecurity
  • Rural areas have less access to nutritious food
  • Traditional foodways are affected by this

Geography, Climate, Food & Flavours

  • November has ideal travel weather: warm and dry
  • Diverse geography: deserts, jungles, volcanoes
  • Food varies by region (coastal = seafood, inland = corn/beans)
  • Cuisine includes salsa, guacamole, tacos, mole
  • Tools: molcajete & metate for grinding spices
  • Common spices: cumin, oregano, epazote, achiote, cinnamon

Culture, Traditions & Attractions

  • Dishes: tacos, mole poblano, tamales, chiles en nogada, pozole
  • Dining habits: 3 meals/day, lunch is largest
  • Table manners: hands visible, finish food, tip 10–15%
  • Día de Muertos = food offerings (pan de muerto)
  • Tourist spots: Chichén Itzá, Zócalo, Cancún
  • Bringing it home: Mole Poblano is memorable, fusion foods like mole poutine
